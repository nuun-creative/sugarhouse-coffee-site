Our Story

More than coffee.
This is community.

Sugar House Coffee has been the heart of the Sugar House neighborhood since 2002. Founded by Bob Evans and carried forward by his family, we're a gathering place where the door is open to everyone, no exceptions.

We locally source our ingredients because supporting our neighbors matters. Our beans are roasted right here in Salt Lake City by Rimini Coffee, our bread comes from Stoneground Bakery, and our pastries are baked by local artisans.

"The community will be there for me and carry me when I can't do it alone."
— Emily Potts, Owner

More Than a Coffee Shop

Rooted in Community

We believe a coffee shop should give back as much as it serves. Here's how we show up for Sugar House.

🏳️‍🌈

Safe Space for All

As a proud member of the Utah LGBTQ+ Chamber of Commerce, we're committed to being a place where every person feels welcome, valued, and safe. We've hosted Fashion Fluid Closet–a free, gender-affirming clothing pop-up–and fly our rainbows proudly year-round.

🎨

Local Artists

Our walls are a rotating gallery of local art. We sponsor the Sugar House Art Walk and give artists a platform to share their work.

🎵

Live Music & Open Mic

Piano players, singer-songwriters, and open mic nights. Grab a latte and discover SLC's next great talent.

🤝

Supporting Local, Always

Rimini Coffee roasts our beans. Stoneground bakes our bread. Local dairies supply our milk. When you buy here, you're supporting a whole network of Salt Lake small businesses. That's been our promise since day one.
